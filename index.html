Three years ago I figured consulting and marketing were two different jobs. Turns out the gap between them is mostly just output format. One ships decks, the other ships campaigns, and both have to be right about something before any of that matters.

Right now I’m doing an MSc in Digital Marketing, interning at a consulting firm, and teaching myself machine learning in whatever hours are left over. That messy overlap is exactly where I want to be.

What actually gets me going is how brands make decisions. Not just what to post or how to grow, but whether the thing they’re building connects to anything real. AI keeps making that question harder to dodge, which is half the reason I like it.
